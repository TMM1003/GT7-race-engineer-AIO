## Car Selection and Rationale

The Ferrari's mid-engine configuration produces more neutral weight distribution and predictable handling, resulting in cleaner separation between key driving phases: braking, cornering, and throttle reapplication. This leads to telemetry signals that are more consistent across laps and drivers, improving the reliability of extracted features such as braking onset, minimum corner speed, and exit speed.

Therefore, the Ferrari 488 GT3 provides a more controlled experimental platform, enabling clearer attribution of performance differences to driver behavior and telemetry representation rather than vehicle-specific instability. This aligns with the study's emphasis on repeatability, structured comparison, and interpretability of learned feature importance.
